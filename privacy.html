Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 2024

The short version: Nook doesn't collect any data. Your notes stay on your device. We don't track you, we don't have analytics, and we don't store anything on servers.

What We Collect

Nothing. Zero. Nada.

Nook is designed with privacy at its core. The app runs entirely on your local machine, and we have no access to:

  • Your notes or any text you write
  • Your personal information
  • Your usage patterns or behavior
  • Your device information
  • Crash reports or diagnostics

Data Storage

All your notes and app preferences are stored locally on your Mac. We don't sync to any cloud services, and we don't have any servers that store your data.

If you uninstall Nook, your local data is removed with the app.

Third-Party Services

Nook doesn't integrate with any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services. We don't share data with anyone because we don't have any data to share.

Updates

Nook checks for updates through GitHub to let you know when a new version is available. This is the only network request the app makes, and it doesn't transmit any personal information.

Your Control

Since all data is stored locally on your device, you have complete control over it. You can:

  • Delete your notes at any time
  • Uninstall the app to remove all data
  • Use the app completely offline

Contact

If you have any questions about this privacy policy, feel free to reach out on GitHub.

We believe privacy is a fundamental right, not a feature. Nook will always be built with this principle in mind.
